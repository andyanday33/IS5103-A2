Limited Accessibility User Groups

Aged web viewers

brief introduction

Firstly, much of the web is not accessible for aged people. Every individual needs the internet to communicate with each other, to search solutions for their working tasks and to address basic information needed. 

Secondly, the accessibility regulations or policies are not enough to guarantee web accessibility. Moreover, even a few websites meet the guidelines, some elder users can still encounter accessible issues.

difficulty

It is widely accepted that the aging abilities of elderly people who have the need of Internet are experiencing age-related impairments such as visual, hearing, physical inconvenience, slower reactivity, cognitive impairments.

For example:   Eyesight declines significantly affecting daily living   estimates:

  • 15.8% of people 65 to 74 years
  • 18.7% of people 75 to 84 years
  • 45.8% of people over 85 years

According to Andrew(2009), it is found that when facing with older users, lots of issues happened related with usable accessibility rather than technical accessibility. These usability issues include aspects such as the presentation of the web content, the inexperience of older people, and the information overloaded often experienced. There is always a need of usable browsers which can accommodate all different kinds of experience, skills and impairments of older users and others in accessing the Web[1].

a case study: older content creators and web accessibility planning 

According to Aqueasha(2020), a case study is mentioned about older content creators and web accessibility planning which is aiming at ensuring access to online content for older adults and people with impairments.    

What is interesting about this case study is that during the accessibility planning process, the older content creators take part in at the very beginning which is an extremely efficient way to take an overview of the webpage whether it is accessible for older people rather than all content and design is already done waiting for refinements to meet the guidelines of web accessibility[2].

existing tools

Specific Guidance on Designing for Older Users

These are guidelines provided by the W3C, providing details which could improve the web accessibility for aged web users.

  • Lists the methods to use WCAG 2.0 to improve the accessibility and usability of websites and web applications for aged end-users.
  • Is arranged by user requirements and WCAG standards.
  • Provides WCAG success criteria and techniques that apply to improving the user experience of older people.
  • Includes references to the Literature Review.
  • More information at: https://www.w3.org/WAI/older-users/#wcag-details

Visually Impaired Users

brief introduction

Firstly, much of the web is not accessible for aged people. Every individual needs the internet to communicate with each other, to search solutions for their working tasks and to address basic information needed. 

Secondly, the accessibility regulations or policies are not enough to guarantee web accessibility. Moreover, even a few websites meet the guidelines, some elder users can still encounter accessibility issues.

Compared with the amount of regular web users without any impairments, the number of blind users seems to be less, however, it is important to raise the concern of providing accessible and usable web service to the blind. It is equal for everyone to have access to and make attributes to the development of Internet.

Because of visual impairment, users usually access the web content by the assistive reading technologies provided by the web browsers and interact with the Internet by touch methods such as the blind keyboard to input. So, it is helpful for the web page designers to let their content accessible and compatible with these technologies which are essential to the blind users.

Difficulty

Web accessibility is always a crucial problem for blind users or users with colour blindness. As a society, it is important to raise concerns and find more tools to improve web accessibility for web viewers with eye impairment.

It is widely known that screen reader is the main technique that help the blind get information from the web page However, this is an approach which is time consuming to navigate the viewers to their desired content.

According to Pauli (2011), there has been a suggestion to divide the web page into different parts logically in which the web viewers could navigate through the logical descriptive title and index numbers according to their own interest. By this method, the web accessibility could be improved by reducing time to help viewers get to the information they need. The whole concept of this method is called IVRS (Interactive Voice Response Systems). By this system, visually impaired users could access the webpage through hearing the index information on their mobile phone and selecting the navigating numbers by touching them on their keypad [1].

assessment system for blind users

According to Alexander (2021), there has been a project with a goal to produce a more efficient and accurate system that aids web accessibility using a sample of webpages. This system would generate a combined accessibility assessment by adding existing tools alongside developer testing. Because of the large-scale need of evaluation of the webpages, efficiency is the key element in the field of testing websites for the blind users [2].

This approach improves automated testing coverage by absorbing the existing evaluative tools and minimising inaccuracy by sampling.

Users With Reduced Mobility

Introduction

People with reduced mobility often encounter a lot of difficulties when accessing the web. They may not be able to move their hands or legs as flexibly as normal people. When using a computer to access the Internet, it may not be possible to complete some specific operations through the keyboard or mouse. For them, the web page is not highly accessible.

Main difficulties

  • Users cannot use mouse or keyboard to type words or search information
  • Windows have many parts, and it can be hard to transfer one page to another page
  • Choosing specific functions or menus in a webpage can be difficult
  • Navigating a webpage by means of scrolling may not be possible

Details and Cases/ exist tools working principle

According to Arias (2016), it is hoped to develop a navigation system based on eye movement and web interaction through eye tracking. The working principle of this plug-in is to complete the required action by capturing the positioning and movement of the user's eyes.

By adding additional navigation bars on both sides of the webpage, the corresponding menus and function options will pop up through the point of view on the screen. The eyes actually act as a mouse, while the gaze acts as a click. Similarly for text input, the plug-in provides an interactive input for the user's eyes on a virtual keyboard. This will greatly enhance the accessibility of the web page to this group of people.

In the cursor interaction, the user controls the point of the cursor by moving the line of sight of the eye. The cursor is in the form of a hollow circle. When the user wants to click a button at a specified position, they only need to stop moving and look at the position, and the hollow circle will gradually be filled with a circle full of color. When the color is full, the click instruction will be triggered to complete the operation.
